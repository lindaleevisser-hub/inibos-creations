CCPP — Contagious Caprine Pleuropneumonia

KKPP / Besmetlike Geitetooglongsiekte
Critical — Emergency Bacterial 🚨 Notifiable disease

One of the most serious goat respiratory diseases worldwide — Mycoplasma capricolum causes acute lung inflammation. Mortality can reach 100% in naive herds. Present in SA.

Notifiable disease. South African law requires that suspected cases be reported to the State Veterinarian immediately.

Symptoms — what to look for

  • Acute respiratory distress
  • High fever
  • Coughing
  • Painful breathing
  • Reluctance to move
  • Death within 1-3 days in acute form
  • Nasal discharge

Causes

  • Mycoplasma capricolum subsp. capripneumoniae
  • Direct contact and aerosol spread
  • Stress — transport, overcrowding
  • Introduced animals most at risk

Treatment

  • Oxytetracycline or tylosin — early treatment
  • Strict isolation
  • Supportive care
  • High mortality even with treatment in acute cases

Prevention

  • Vaccination where available
  • Strict quarantine of new animals
  • Biosecurity — dedicated equipment per group
  • Avoid stress and overcrowding
Key point: NOTIFIABLE. Any acute respiratory disease killing goats rapidly — report to State Vet. Goats hide illness even more than other animals.
